Why Tinylab?

Across agriculture, biomanufacturing, and chemistry (ABC), outside labs still gate quality control—slow, expensive, and too far from where decisions get made.

01

Context

1.9 Million US farms & facilities, producing $8T/yr in ABC goods, rely on 3rd Party labs every day for...

  • Regulatory compliance
  • Quality control
  • Process monitoring

Case Study: Industrial Fermentation

Microbial fermentation processes are highly sensitive to small changes in temperature, chemical composition, agitation, and more.

The best way to monitor the health of the microbes and progress of the fermentation is through chemical sampling and analysis.

This means getting chemical information quickly is essential for making live adjustments to the fermentation process to maximize yields.

High-end process probes and analytical instruments are prohibitively expensive and 48+ hour wait times for results from 3rd-party labs prevent immediate action.

02

Problem

These labs are too slow and too expensive. This comes at the cost of...

  • Increased risk of spoilage and loss
  • Decrease operational efficiency
  • Can’t collect enough useful data to increase yields
03

Solution

Tinylab — the automated desktop lab.

Anyone. Anywhere. Full QC on your bench.

Cost

Wait
48+ hrs → <1 hr
04

Evolution

Tinylab users can improve their production & growing processes in a simulated environment, the same way that modern manufacturers do with robots.

  • Reduce expenses

  • Improve quality

How Tinylab works...

Tinylab is the world’s smallest analytical laboratory—a full workflow on one benchtop system.

01

Sample prep

Microfluidic mixing, chemical derivatization, and serial dilution—handled automatically before analysis begins.

02

Separation

Thermal processing and chromatography to isolate what you need to measure.

03

Measurement

10 unique on-board instruments specializing in various applications of Spectroscopy, Voltammetry, and electrochemistry facilitate all sample testing.

04

Inference & Analysis

Lightning-fast AI inference turns signal into concentrations you can act on.
