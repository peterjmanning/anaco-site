What Makes TinyLab Work

Modular components for field and line analysis — autosampler through photonics.

Autosampler

28
  • Up to 28 samples at a time
  • Available filters
  • Available liquefaction module
  • Self-cleaning mode

Microreactors

24
  • Headspace pressure & gas control
  • Microfluidic mixing: Up to 0.1 μL accuracy
  • In-situ electrochemical measurement
    • DPV (Differential Pulse Voltammetry)
    • CV (Cyclic Voltammetry)
    • pH
    • TA (Titratable Acidity)
  • Independent temperature control zones: −10 to +125°C (+14 to +257°F)
  • Self-cleaning mode

Programmable Temperature Vaporizer

  • Heats up to +300°C
  • Modes:
    • Quick Ramp
    • Programmed Ramp Schedule
  • Split gas control: 1:1, 1:2, 1:3, 1:4, 1:9, 1:19, 1:99 (Outlet:Waste)

Gas Chromatography Columns

4×
  • 4 parallel MEMS μGC columns
  • Unique stationary phases
  • On-chip heating/cooling

Sensing Modules

  • Flow-Modulated Pneumatic Resonator
    • Use a single spectrometer for multiple measurements
    • Control & divert gas flow
  • MEMS multi-pass optical flow cells
    • Gas: UV-VIS, NIR
    • Liquid: UV-VIS, NIR
  • MEMS single-pass optical flow cells
    • Liquid: UV-VIS, NIR

Multiple Photonics Options Available

  • Spectrometer options:
    • 200–1000 nm
    • 900–1700 nm
    • 900–2500 nm

The UCLA NanoLab provides access to advanced research infrastructure for fabrication and characterization of nanoscale materials, systems and devices. The NPC Technology Center offers a curated collection of high-end scientific instrumentation to enable the physicochemical characterization of nanoscale materials, devices, and systems under a wide range of experimental conditions. Capabilities include surface analysis by Scanning Probe Microscopy (SPM), hydrodynamic size and surface charge analysis by Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS) and Zeta Potential (ZP) measurements, and elemental and chemical analysis by ICP-MS.

The California Nanosystems Institute (CNSI) and Leica Microsystems, Inc. have combined efforts to establish the Leica Microsystems Center of Excellence (CoE) at UCLA.

The Advanced Light Microscopy and Spectroscopy (ALMS) laboratories provide a unique collection of high-end, customized fluorescence microscopes, small-animal imaging devices, and fluorescent probes to perform fluorescence-based measurements at various spatial (nm to cm), temporal (ns to days) and spectral (UV–NIR) ranges.
