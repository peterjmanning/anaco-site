A Letter from Our CEO

Hello friend, I'm Benjamin Liker, the inventor of Tinylab and founder of Anaco.

I started Anaco because like most Americans, I'm fed up with the U.S. food system. I believe that there are hundreds of millions of people who can (and will) make better decisions for their health, but only if they're given the opportunity to know exactly what they're putting in their bodies. Take this example: If you buy a carton of eggs from Whole Foods and a carton of eggs from a local farmer's market, they're going to look and taste different. Despite both being labeled as "Eggs," they have completely different chemical breakdowns. Two strawberry bushes with identical DNA in the same field might have different sugar levels because the valley they're in heats unevenly. Two oranges picked from the same tree might have different levels of citric acid because of differences in sun exposure.

You get the point — and under the current system, there's simply no way to know exactly what you're putting in your body! The crazy part is that this isn't due to outdated regulations nor a corporate conspiracy to poison us (despite that being a politically convenient hypothesis) — it's the result of cutting-edge technology and market forces in action… and the problems go far beyond food testing.

Over the past half-century, our society has poured a LOT of money into medical and biotechnology research; as a result, laboratory equipment manufacturers have increasingly focused on making highly-capable, but incredibly complex and expensive instruments for those researchers. However, those instruments are overkill for the analytical QC testing labs, which perform repetitive compositional testing on things like food, wine, beverages, chemicals, fuels, and more. The result? Obscenely high lab start-up costs, industry consolidation, high operational costs, and inefficient processes — all combining to result in prices of $500–5000 per sample and wait times of 2–3 days. At that price and pace, it's financially and logistically impossible to test anything at scale.

That's why I invented Tinylab. It uses state-of-the-art technologies to replicate the functions of traditional analytical instruments in a much more compact package that's cheaper, more energy efficient, requires less maintenance, and uses fewer reagents than other instruments. Its operating principle is to automate the 4 critical steps to any chemical analysis: Sample Preparation, Component Separation, Detection, and Data Analysis. Just a few years ago, none of these were possible at the price point we're offering Tinylab, but due to recent advancements in microfabrication, additive manufacturing, photonics, and machine learning/AI, we have created something truly wonderful.

When I travelled across the country talking to farmers, vintners, facility managers, business owners, and plain ol' ordinary folks, I realized that most people who would get the most use out of a Tinylab don't have the extensive education needed to operate typical analytical tools. Because of this, we've designed Tinylab to be remarkably easy to use. You control it with an iPad, selecting only what tests you want to run on your samples and how fast you want the results. They say you can only choose 2 of: Cheap, Good, Fast. Tinylab, already the most inexpensive tool of its kind, allows you to choose between more accurate (but slower) analysis and "quick and dirty" measurements. It has self-cleaning modes and uses the absolute minimum quantity of consumables. It automatically alerts you if there is a problem with the device, and we swap out the problematic unit at no cost.

What's more astounding is that Tinylab gets better the more you use it. Our proprietary algorithm learns your processes, what analyses you find important, and what inputs drive quantifiable results for your products. After about 2 years of use, we are able to create what's called a Chemical Digital Twin (CDT), on which you can run virtual simulations of various process improvements; typically used in manufacturing, this is the first time CDTs (and their benefits) will be available to the dozens of industries using Tinylab. They allow you to improve the 3 most critical things to any Agricultural, Biomanufacturing, or Chemicals-related business: Quality, Cost, and Yield. And even better news: The Chemical Digital Twins trained on your processes is your intellectual property, not anyone else's.

Tinylab is a product that comes from the heart, and I hope you enjoy using it as much as we enjoy making it.

Sincerely,

Benjamin Liker
CEO & Founder, Anaco
